Debussy's Tonal Ambiguity

Year: 2021
Class: Digital Musicology
University: EPFL


Arthur Rackham (1909). Undine. From here



Claude Debussy reformed the traditional paradigm of func3 tional tonality. He achieved a feat of floating and elusive 4 vagueness in his compositions, that this paper refers to as 5 ambiguous tonality. Debussy’s piano music evolved from 6 tonal clarity to a tonally ambiguous harmony in his later 7 pieces. The aim of this paper is to study this shift us8 ing computational methods. We hypothesise that the shift 9 is characterised by an increased use of symmetric struc10 tures, fragmented forms and pentatonic rather than diatonic 11 scales. Wavescapes, a method based on Discrete Fourier 12 Transform (DFT), is the central tool of this project to visu13 alise and analyse tonal hierarchies in musical pieces. The 14 harmonic structure of several piano pieces of Debussy are 15 analysed using wavescapes, acknowledging their strengths 16 and limitations. We propose some statistical methods that 17 are designed specifically for the task, to validate and prob18 lematise our hypotheses. The results are in line with the 19 initial hypotheses, allowing to computationally define De20 bussy’s tonal ambiguity and visualise its development in 21 time.

Debussy. Undine. Pitch Scapes


In short, the intent of this visualization is to exploit the possibilities opened up by the Subject index on Modern and Contemporary art. As art belonging to the Avantgardes of these last two centuries witnesses often a detachment from forms and colors, the contenutistic and symbolic aspects of this art become progressively less directly associated with the forms and colors used to depict them. By presenting artworks organized by their index (and therefore their content), this project aims to stimulate the user into understanding what forms and colors are distinctive of certain contents. It aims to do so for Avantgarde art, as this association of forms and colors to contents is weak.
