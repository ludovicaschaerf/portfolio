‘Mit und Gegen’ by Wassily Kandinsky:
The Two Levels of an Artwork

Year: 2019
Class: Philosophical Problems
University: Amsterdam University College


Vasily Kandinsky (1929). Mit und Gegen. Retrieved from: here



Kandinsky has always been my favourite artist of the XX century. When the occasion came, I wrote this essay about the aesthetics of his art. Below is the abstract and the full text:

In his essay ‘Beauty’ (2003), Nick Zangwill presents a number of issues and distinctions in the field of aesthetics. This essay focuses on the distinction that was delineated by Kant between free and dependent beauty. Zangwill explains it as follows: ‘The free beauty of a thing is independent of its history (and indeed of its future), whereas a thing's dependent beauty depends on its history in so far as that history enters into its function.’ (Zangwill, p.8) In short, by free beauty he means the beauty of the object in itself, while by dependent beauty he intends the teleological beauty, that is linked to its purpose. Zangwill proceeds in his analysis by presenting his ‘primacy conjecture’. According to him, free beauty is at the basis of the possibility of an aesthetic experience: it is only when we are able to appreciate the free beauty of some entity that we can appreciate the dependent one. This essay proposes a modified distinction between free and dependent beauty. It claims the necessity to widen the definition of dependent beauty by including also intellectual beauty - by which I mean the beauty that arises from acquiring knowledge about the ‘history’ of the object in consideration. Further, it evaluates the validity of the ‘primacy conjecture’, reflecting on the role of imagination in each of the two levels of beauty defined by Kant.
