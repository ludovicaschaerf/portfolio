AI Priest

Year: 2021
Class: Artistic Practices II
University: EPFL - HEAD Geneva


Diocesi di Mantova. Priest giving a mass in Diocesi di Mantova. Voice over with AI.


Can AI help popularize the liturgy, extending its understanding to sporadic churchgoers? Is an AI summary more unbiased than a human one? Would this ‘solve’ the issue of interpretation? Can AI interact with the Sacred? Would this be profane? Will the vast use of AI distance us from reality? For example, in a future where we will read mostly texts produced by AIs, will we be further away from reality? Will we be able to trust a summary by an AI, especially if we cannot read the full text to double-check for ourselves? Why do we think that it is appropriate to apply AI to so many areas? Why are people so hyped by it that apply it with great trust even if they do not understand how and why it works?

This project consists of a the Substack newsletter feed of 'Religion&Tech'. The feed constains elements that concur to create a reflective environment on the intersection between Artificial Intelligence and Religion. Being the liturgy still heavily reliant on out-of-context readings of the Bible, understanding becomes problematic for people who do not attend every Sunday Mass or cannot read the full Scriptures. In addition, after the readings, the priest begins his Homily, giving an interpretation of the mass and the readings. This often is extremely allegorical and abstract. In this issue, and many others, Artificial Intelligence can come to the rescue of Religion. AI can summarize what comes before the readings in the Scriptures, and it can do so without the allegorical or personal interpretations of the priests. The two explanations (the AI and the priest-made ones) can therefore complement each other and provide believers with the tools they need. The reflective environment is therefore intended to stimulate a reflection on the use of an AI-made summary of some passages of the Bible before the liturgy. Consequently, the project envisages an Italian priest opening a newsletter in 2030 (2050) to suggest his idea: to post, every Saturday, a summary of the events preceding the readings of the following Sunday mass.

These summaries are be made by AIs for efficiency reasons and to provide an unbiased and objective summary of the piece. Further, these are enacted on a video of a mass, where the priest's voice is substituted by an AI-generated voice reading the summary made by AI. With this goal in mind, the priest publishes a second newsletter. This time showing a real prototype of of what he has in mind by that. He publishes a video of the priest of the Diocesi di Mantova, whose audio has been substituted with the neural voice created with voicemaker . The words read by the neural voice are a summary of the beginning of the 24th chapter of Luke's Gospel that has been summarized by quillbot..

Ludovica Schaerf. Third and last post of the AI priest including attachement.


The priest, after months of activity in the newsletter, suggests his final vision. He envisages a new perspective for AI: the creation of new words of God. Using deepai text generator, the priest creates a new ending of the passage summarized in the second newsletter. The priest is now completely hyped about AI and cannot see its limits any longer. He believes that the new means of communication of God can only be through AI, which will be able to write Godly words as it is not tainted by human sin.
